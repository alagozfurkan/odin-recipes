Kuru Fasulye

Description

Contrary to belief, the national dish of Turkey is not Kebab, Lahmacun or Menemen. Instead, many locals will name Kuru Fasulye, a fiery stew of white beans that have been slow-cooked in a rich tomato, pepper and chilli broth.

Ingredients

  • 320 g dried white beans (haricot)
  • 1 ¾ tbsp salt (24g)
  • 00 ml extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 onions
  • 1 heaped tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 heaped tbsp Biber şalçası
  • Freshly steamed rice & Turkish pickles to serve

Steps

  1. Add the beans and 1 tbsp (14g) of the salt to a mixing bowl, cover them generously with water and soak overnight.
  2. Stir in the tomato paste, pepper paste and black pepper and continue frying until the pastes have dissolved into the oil.
  3. Carefully pour in the water to stop the frying, then drain the beans, rinse them and add to the casserole as well.
  4. Trim the chillis and green pepper, cut the green pepper into chunks and add both to the casserole.
  5. Bring it to a boil, then cover it with a lid and simmer for 75 minutes, stirring every now and then, until the beans are tender.
  6. Once tender, season them with the remaining ¾ tbsp (10g) of salt and continue cooking for another 10 minutes to let the beans absorb the salt.
  7. Divide over plates and serve alongside freshly steamed rice and Turkish pickles.
